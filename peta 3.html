LUZON:

Mayon Volcano is an active stratovolcano in the Philippines famous for its perfectly symmetrical cone shape. Taal Volcano is another highly active volcano in the Philippines known for its unique geology and scenic beauty. Rizal Park, also popularly known as Luneta Park, is a historic 58-hectare urban oasis located in Ermita, Manila, adjacent to the old walled city of Intramuros. The Hundred Islands National Park is a protected area in Alaminos, Pangasinan, featuring 124 mushroom-shaped islets scattered across the Lingayen Gulf. Baguio City is a highly urbanized mountain resort city in northern Luzon, Philippines, celebrated as the country's "Summer Capital" for its cool climate and pine-dominated scenery.

VISAYAS:

Chocolate Hills is a unique geological formation in Bohol consisting of at least 1,260 symmetrical green hills that turn cocoa-brown during the dry season. Boracay is a small, world-famous tropical island in Aklan province renowned for its powdery white-sand beaches, vibrant nightlife, and crystal-clear waters. Kawasan Falls is a stunning multi-tier waterfall system in Badian, Cebu, famous for its intense turquoise waters and popular canyoneering adventures. Kalanggaman Island is a pristine, uninhabited islet in Leyte famous for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and a stunning, long white-sand sandbar that stretches out into the sea. Siquijor is an island province in the Central Visayas region known for its scenic white beaches, centuries-old churches, and mystical folklore involving traditional healers and witchcraft.

MINDANAO:

Siargao is a tear-shaped island in Surigao del Norte widely recognized as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," famous for its world-class Cloud 9 wave, coconut palm forests, and laid-back island vibe. Great Santa Cruz Island in Zamboanga City is a protected marine reserve famous for its unique pink sand beach, which gets its blush color from pulverized red organ-pipe coral mixed with white sand. Camiguin is an island province known as the "Island Born of Fire" due to its seven volcanoes, featuring unique landmarks like the Sunken Cemetery, White Island sandbar, and natural hot springs. The Hinatuan Enchanted River in Surigao del Sur is a deep, crystal-clear spring river famed for its mesmerizing deep-blue hues, mysterious underground caves, and daily fish-feeding rituals. Dahican Beach is a crescent-shaped coastline in Mati, Davao Oriental, renowned for its powerful swells that make it a premier destination for skimboarding and surfing, as well as a sanctuary for sea turtles.
